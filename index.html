About us

Movie Artists & Workers Association (MAWA)

MAWA is a dynamic organization dedicated to supporting and empowering individuals across all fields of cinema. We stand by our members, striving for their betterment and collective success within the film industry.

Our Mission

We aim to foster unity, creativity, and collaboration among cinema professionals. Our mission is to provide essential support, advocacy, and opportunities for our diverse membership base.

Our Services

Membership Programs

MAWA offers structured membership programs catering to individuals working in various fields of cinema, including actors, production crew, technicians, and more.

Membership benefits include access to collective film projects, networking opportunities, skill development programs, and support services.

Support Programs

MAWA runs support programs aimed at assisting low-income members with essential needs such as medical assistance, housing schemes, and government life plans

The "Kurtu Kai Thang" project is implemented to provide housing solutions to homeless members, demonstrating MAWA's commitment to member welfare.

Collective Film Projects

MAWA organizes and facilitates collaborative film projects where members can participate and contribute their skills

These projects not only showcase members' talents but also provide practical experience and exposure within the industry.

Skill Development

MAWA offers workshops, seminars, and training sessions focused on skill development and career advancement within the film industry.

Members have access to resources and educational opportunities to enhance their craft and stay updated with industry trends.

Industry Connections

Membership with MAWA provides networking opportunities with fellow professionals and industry stakeholders.

Members can leverage these connections to collaborate on projects, seek career opportunities, and build lasting relationships within the film community.

Advocacy and Representation

MAWA advocates for the rights and interests of its members, including fair wages, working conditions, and opportunities for career growth.

The organization represents its members' collective voice in discussions with industry bodies, policymakers, and other relevant stakeholders.
